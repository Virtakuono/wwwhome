A story of a McKinley drinking bladder

I was in Intersport a few days ago, with a friend. She considered buying a McKinley backpack. I told her to get a good backpack instead, trying to share my wisdom that in the woods you really need a good backpack and good shoes, those are not the items to save money on. Well, she took my advise and left the backpack on the shelf. I, on the other hand, bought a drinking bladder and gave myself a lesson reminding not to use McKinley products even on something less critical like a drinking bladder.

Long story short, the closing mechanism of the bladder is a disaster. On the second use, it broke. I have used Source bladders before, and apparently the guys at McKinley tried to copy the closing mechanism from the Source bladders. Source bladders have a plastic rod that is attached to the bladder, the mouth of the bladder rolls around the rod and an external piece of plastic locks the whole thing in place. For images of the mechanism, see here. The McKinley guys copied the idea, but the plastic rod is not fixed in place, resulting in the slippery PVC sliding through the locking mechanism when the bladder is hanging in the backpack on the bladder holder.
