A story of a McKinley drinking bladder

I was in Intersport a few days ago, with a friend. She considered buying a McKinley backpack. I told her to get a good backpack instead, trying to share my wisdom that in the woods you really need a good backpack and good shoes, those are not the items to save money on. Well, she took my advise and left the backpack on the shelf. I, on the other hand, bought a drinking bladder and gave myself a lesson reminding not to use McKinley products even on something less critical like a drinking bladder.

Long story short, the closing mechanism of the bladder is a disaster. On the second use, it broke. I have used Source bladders before, and apparently the guys at McKinley tried to copy the closing mechanism from the Source bladders. Source bladders have a plastic rod that is attached to the bladder, the mouth of the bladder rolls around the rod and an external piece of plastic locks the whole thing in place. For images of the mechanism, see here. The McKinley guys copied the idea, but the plastic rod is not fixed in place, resulting in the slippery PVC sliding through the locking mechanism when the bladder is hanging in the backpack on the bladder holder.

Savotta Trooper

I tend to use quite a bit of military surplus stuff, mainly for the reason that it tends to be quite durable. Militaries test their kit quite extensively and anything used by the military has to go through extensive testing. Unfortunately some companies have noticed this and chosen to use their fame as a military supplier to sell cheaper crap quality items while giving the buyer the impression that the goods are military grade. Savotta is known as a supplier of the Finnish armed forces and I am sad to notice that they have chosen to take their fame and sell to civilians products that are from a different planet than the goods sold to the military.

The Savotta Trooper satchel is a sad example of poor quality stuff from a renowned manufacturer and probably an example of how one can get pretty much everything wrong in a simple item like a satchel:
1. The stitching of the flap is off and as a result, the velcro attachment of the flap does not attach properly.
2. The material of the bag is cordura lined with pvc, with the lining falling off after a few weeks of use.
After the lining falls off, it is just a matter of time before the bag has a hole in it. 3. The PALS straps are of the cheapest kind and start to wear out within months.
4. The transparent film in the map pouch is punctured in no time, even in summertime use.
5. There is a transparent compartment for an ID card that works as a sure way of losing your ID.
6. In some bags, the nylon straps are sewn to the body of the bag with a patch of nylon on the flipside of the bag for durable stitching, not in Savotta Trooper. As a result, the shoulder strap stitching will start to fail in a matter of weeks.
